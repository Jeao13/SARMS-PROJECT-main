RAD 140 (Testolone)

Introduction

If you’re looking for a comprehensive guide on RAD 140 (also referred to as Testolone), you’ve come to the right place. We’ll discuss everything you need to know about this SARM, from its recommended dosage to its benefits and corresponding side effects. Find out if this is for you, read on!

What’s Testolone?

As mentioned, the RAD 140 or Testolone is a form of a Selective Androgen Receptor Modulator (SARM). Currently, Testolone is under research development and clinical trials to become the medical answer for breast cancer and other muscle-wasting illnesses.

Testolone has been getting famous among professional athletes, including bodybuilders, primarily for its evident benefits in muscle strengthening and mass build-up. Below, we’re going to talk about this SARM further.

How does Testolone Work?

The way that Testolone works can be compared on how steroid compounds do; however, RAD140 selectively chooses the androgen receptors in your system and binds to them. The opposite process happens with steroids because they don’t have a specific target to attach on, leading to more side effects.

Simply put, the RAD 140 focuses on binding to androgen receptors primarily found in your skeleton and muscle tissues; this means that the benefits of this SARM is more prominent on gaining muscle mass and losing fat.

Is Testolone Legal?

RAD140 is currently legal to purchase and use globally, except for Australia. Because it’s still undergoing research and trials, you’d notice that it’s categorized as a “Research Chemical” and “Not for Human Consumption” as it’s not yet approved by the FDA. Once its studies and clinical trials are finished, Testolone would be FDA-approved.

In Australia, you can’t buy or use Testolone unless you have doctor’s prescription; however, lots of Australians are already using this with or without prescription.

When it comes to sports, the RAD140 is banned because of its evident advantages on muscle mass and strength. This SARM is strictly prohibited in most sports events, including the Olympics. Additionally, Testolone is forbidden by the United States Anti Doping Agency (USADA) and the World Anti Doping Agency (WADA). Never use this substance if you’re part of a professional sports event.
